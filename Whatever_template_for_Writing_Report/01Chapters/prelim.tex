Let \begin{equation}
  \omega:=e^{2\pi i /n}
\end{equation} be the $n$th root of unity. We then have the following identity:
\begin{equation}
  \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} w^k(b-a)=n\delta(a,b)
\end{equation}
This identity can be written as: 
$$M=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & \cdots \\ 1 & \omega^{-1} & \omega^{-2} & \cdots \\ 1 & \omega^{-2} & \omega^{-4} & \cdots \\ \vdots & \vdots &\vdots & \ddots
 \end{bmatrix} $$
 Is invertible with an inverse: 
 $$M^{-1}=\frac{1}{n}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 1 & \cdots \\ 1 & \omega & \omega^2 & \cdots \\ 1 & \omega^2 & \omega^4 & \cdots \\ \vdots & \vdots &\vdots & \ddots
 \end{bmatrix} $$
